Tinmen and flesh robots

A puff of dark yellow smoke slowly left a pair of cracked and shivering lips. For the second time this morning, Agent M82 looked with a scowl at his branded JoyPuffs© and wondered why he had even bought the damn thing. He was freezing, and a few puffs of a cheap cig would have soothed him wonderfully, but of course an agent of Smile couldn't be caught with something as brand-unfriendly as that.

The detective snuggled tighter in his wool coat -- no fancy Warmth and Confort© suits for the likes of him -- and continued marching on toward the factory.

He made a crunch with each step of his heavy boots, just loud enough to be heard above the howling of the wind.

Agent M82 arrived a few minutes later in front of the factory's heavy metal doors. He put his JoyPuffs© away in his left breast pocket, and quickly surveyed his surrounding. The snow fresh and bore no footprints and even looked fresh, not unusual by itself given the recent blizzard. A pile of snow was stacked in front of the gargantuan doors, only slightly higher than it should be given the supposed industrial traffic.

A gale stronger than the ones before swept over him, leading him to briefly pat his coat to look for cigs before catching himself and cursing. Unhurried about trying to contact the workers inside, he did his due diligence and made a quick tour around the facility. The giant mass of metal plates and bolts was coal black, in sharp contrast with the snowy landscape it was lost in. It stood at least a few hundred meters high, and was spread as far as the horizon. On top of the black mountain of a building sprawled a forest of chimneys, spewing columns of dark fumes that rose high in the sky. The detective looked at them, and smirked in self derision at how much he was envying their warmth. Despite looking around, he couldn't locate any windows or secondary exits, giving the factory the air of a sealed tomb. He reckoned a Fullfilment rehability center© wouldn't have looked out of place here.

While walking back toward the entrance, he briefly wondered if the facility had any snow mobiles he would be able to borrow so as to avoid making the trek back. It had been a few hours since he had last seen any other human settlement, he wasn't excited about braving potential blizards again.

Agent M82 carefully considered the poltry crewman before him. He seemed especially jolly, and had an assured vigor to his stride that contended with his crackling voice. Mattew noticed him shifting uncomfortably in his bright orange uniform, and readjusting his safety helmet. There was a surprising lack of familiarity to his gestures.

"So, are you sure there is no problem to report here", inquired the inspector.

"N-n-no chief, nothing to report. I don't know why the big shots at HQ bothered to send you in little frozen home"

Agent M82 clutched on his wounded leg, hoping he wounldn't bleed out to death too soon. He heard the abomination crawling toward him with its remaining arm. Gripping his Lullaby© gun tighter than before, he rose on his uninjured leg with a wince. The detective lined the thing's head with his visor, his vision spinning from blood loss. The monster was still a few meters away, but he could hear its claws ripping into the steel floor with each lunge forward. The grinding noises as loud as if it was inches away from his ears. After a deep breath, he finally got its skull in the sight line, and shot at it. The bullet blasted it with a splash, painting the corridor with red goo and metal shards.
